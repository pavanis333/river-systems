## Key Coverage

### Important Features
- **Panch Prayags**: Vishnu, Nand, Karna, Rudra, Dev (Mnemonic: Vishnu Ne Karna ko Rasta Dikhaya)
- **Drainage Pattern**: 77% Bay of Bengal, 23% Arabian Sea
- **Drainage Divide**: Delhi Ridge-Aravallis-Vindhyas-Satpuras-Western Ghats
- **Age**: Himalayan rivers (40-60 mn yrs) vs Peninsular (200 mn yrs)
- **Classification**: Major (>20,000 sq km), Medium (2000-20,000), Minor (<2000)

### Famous Lakes
- **Wular Lake**: India's largest freshwater lake (Jhelum, Kashmir)
- **Kanwar Tal**: One of India's biggest oxbow lakes (Burhi Gandak, Bihar)

### Notable Facts
- **Kosi**: "Sorrow of Bihar" - shifting course, 7 tributaries (Sapt Kosi)
- **National Waterway 1**: Ganga (Haldia to Prayagraj)
- **Ghaghara**: Antecedent river from Tibet
- **Beas**: Only Indus tributary entirely in India
- **Panjnad**: Combined name for 5 Indus tributaries (all left bank)
- **Gorges**: Indus near Nanga Parbat, Beas at Kati & Largi
- **Shyok**: From Rimo Glacier (Siachen tongue)
- **Damodar**: Tributary of Hooghly (distributary of Ganga)

### Important Passes & Origins
- **Shipki La**: Sutlej entry to India
- **Baralacha La**: Chenab origin
- **Rohtang Pass**: Beas (Beas Kund), Ravi (west of)
- **Namcha Barwa**: Brahmaputra entry to India
- **Verinag**: Jhelum origin
- **Gangotri & Satopanth**: Ganga source glaciers
- **Yamunotri**: Yamuna source
- **Amarkantak**: Son, Narmada, Johila (radial drainage)

## Study Tips

1. Start with the three major Himalayan systems
2. Focus on Panch Prayags and their mnemonic
3. Remember left vs right bank tributaries
4. Learn important origins (glaciers, passes)
5. Special features (Kosi shifting, Wular Lake, Kanwar Tal)
6. Drainage divide and percentage split (77-23)
7. River age differences (Himalayan vs Peninsular)
